## What's Inside

In 2026, Judge Rakoff (S.D.N.Y.) ruled in US v. Heppner that conversations with AI tools are not protected by attorney-client privilege and can be subpoenaed. This guide explains what that means in practice — and what your options are if you discuss sensitive information in AI tools.

**You'll get:**
- What the ruling actually covers — and who should actually change their behavior
- Option 1: Enterprise AI plans with zero data retention (Claude Enterprise, ChatGPT Enterprise) — cost and how they work
- Option 2: Local AI (Gemma 4 on iPhone, LM Studio, Ollama) — free options with nothing on servers
- Option 3: Self-hosted AI on your own infrastructure — for larger teams
- A quick decision table matching your situation to the right option

## Who This Is For

Business owners, consultants, lawyers, real estate agents, healthcare providers, and anyone who discusses client work, deals, disputes, or confidential strategy in AI tools. For casual personal use — writing help, cooking questions — this ruling almost certainly doesn't affect you.

The guide is honest about this: most people don't need to change anything. But if you fall into the category that does, this tells you exactly what to do.

## The Simple Version

If your AI conversations appeared in court, would you be comfortable? If yes, keep using what you're using. If no, pick one of the three options in this guide. The free option (Gemma 4 local) takes 10 minutes to set up.
