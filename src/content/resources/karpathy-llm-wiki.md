## What's Inside

On April 3, 2026, Andrej Karpathy posted about using LLMs to build personal knowledge bases — and it went massively viral. His system: dump raw research into a folder, run Claude Code, get a fully interlinked wiki with 100+ articles that maintains itself. No RAG, no databases, no manual organizing.

This guide adapts that exact system for content creators.

**You'll get:**
- **The folder structure** that works for transcripts, ideas, research notes, and saved articles
- **The two Claude commands** — compilation (new material → wiki articles) and linting (weekly health check)
- **Copy-paste prompts** for both operations
- **4 creator-specific use cases** — from script research to series planning to team knowledge bases

## Who This Is For

Content creators who have good research habits but bad organization habits. If you have a Notes app full of ideas you never use, a Downloads folder full of articles you saved but forgot, or transcripts from videos you watched but didn't fully process — this system turns all of it into something you can actually search, navigate, and use. Karpathy shared the full setup as a free GitHub gist. This guide shows you how to run it as a creator, not a developer.
